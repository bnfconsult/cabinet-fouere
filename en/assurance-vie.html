General Overview

The firm handles disputes arising from life insurance contracts, particularly when difficulties arise between policyholders, beneficiaries, insurers or intermediaries.

These disputes may involve policy management, interpretation of the beneficiary clause, or the liability of professionals involved in the subscription or management of the contract.

Disputes with Insurers

Certain situations may lead to a dispute with the insurance company. Such disputes may notably involve:

Refusal to Pay

Refusal by the insurer to pay out the capital, challenge to the right to payment, or blocking of the policy.

Clause Interpretation

Interpretation of contract clauses, particularly in cases of ambiguity or unilateral modification.

Surrender Conditions

Conditions for policy surrender or management, disputed fees, unauthorised arbitrages.

Payment Delays

Payment deadlines or conditions, unjustified delays in the settlement of benefits.

The firm intervenes to analyse the contractual situation and determine the available courses of action.

Beneficiary Clause Challenges

Disputes relating to the beneficiary clause are common in life insurance matters. They may notably involve:

Interpretation

Analysis of the clause when its wording is ambiguous or incomplete.

Modification

Designation of the beneficiary, conditions and validity challenges.

Challenges

Disputes between heirs and beneficiaries, manifestly excessive premiums.

Validity

Acceptance of the benefit, revocability of the clause, conditions of validity.

These situations often require an in-depth legal analysis of the contract and the estate context.
