General Overview

Financial fraud in the insurance sector can take various forms and cause considerable harm to policyholders and beneficiaries.

The firm intervenes when insurance intermediaries, financial advisers or wealth managers have committed fraudulent acts to the detriment of their clients.

The firm assists clients on both the civil and criminal fronts, in coordination with the relevant authorities when necessary.

Types of Fraud Encountered

Financial fraud in the insurance sector may take the following forms in particular:

Premium Embezzlement

Collection of premiums by the intermediary without remitting them to the insurance company, resulting in the client having no effective coverage.

Document Falsification

Forged signatures, altered beneficiary clauses, fabrication of fraudulent contracts or false certificates.

Fraud and Breach of Trust

Fraudulent schemes, promises of fictitious returns, misuse of funds entrusted by clients.

Identity Theft

Fraudulent use of a client's identity to subscribe to contracts, modify beneficiaries or carry out surrenders.

Legal Remedies

In the face of financial fraud, several legal avenues may be pursued depending on the nature of the facts:

In-depth Analysis

Detailed examination of the situation, reconstruction of the chronology of events and precise identification of responsibilities.

Case File Preparation

Methodical gathering of evidence: contractual documents, correspondence, bank statements and witness testimony.

Civil Action

Initiation of liability proceedings to obtain full compensation for the financial and moral damage suffered.

Criminal Action

Filing of a criminal complaint and constitution as a civil party when the facts constitute criminal offences: fraud, breach of trust.

Frequently Asked Questions

Several warning signs may indicate fraud: absence of official documents from the insurance company, inconsistent statements, inability to contact the insurer directly, promises of abnormally high returns. The firm can analyse your situation and identify indicators of fraud.

In case of embezzlement, it is possible to bring a civil liability action against the intermediary and, where applicable, against the insurance company. A criminal complaint may also be filed for breach of trust or fraud.

In certain situations, the insurance company's liability may be engaged, particularly when it has failed in its duty to supervise or monitor its intermediaries.

Limitation periods vary depending on the legal qualification of the facts. In criminal matters, the limitation period generally runs from the discovery of the offence. It is essential to act promptly to preserve your rights.

Recovery of funds depends on the solvency of the perpetrator of the fraud and the existing guarantees. The firm implements all protective measures and enforcement procedures to maximise the chances of recovery.
