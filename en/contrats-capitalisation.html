Issues in Case of Loss or Theft

In the event of loss or theft of a capitalisation contract or bearer bond, several legal questions arise immediately.

The issuing institution, the type of contract and the subscription documents must be identified. The circumstances of the disappearance, the date of discovery and evidence of prior possession must be documented.

Risks and Evidence

The loss or theft of a bearer bond presents specific risks that must be anticipated and managed methodically.

Risk of Presentation by a Third Party

A bearer bond belongs to the person who physically holds it. Its loss or theft may result in a payment claim being made by another person.

Evidence

Subscription documents, statements and certificates issued by the financial institution, correspondence and testimony establishing ownership.

Deadlines and Procedures

Time limit for requesting payment, specific judicial procedure, steps with the issuer to block or oppose payment.

Opposition to Payment

Protective measures to prevent payment to a third party and safeguard the rights of the true owner of the bond.

Frequently Asked Questions

A bearer capitalisation bond is a financial instrument issued by a financial institution or insurance company. Unlike registered contracts, the right to payment belongs to the person who physically holds the bond.

In principle, the holder of the bond may request its settlement from the issuing institution. Presentation of the original document is generally required to obtain payment.

When a capitalisation bond is lost, it is important to promptly inform the issuing institution, gather the documents that establish the existence of the bond, and examine the possible steps to preserve the holder's rights. In certain situations, a specific procedure may be necessary to obtain payment of the bond.

In case of theft, it may be advisable to report the theft to the relevant authorities, inform the issuing institution of the bond, and examine the legal remedies available to prevent payment to a third party.

Payment of a bearer bond is generally conditional upon presentation of the original document. However, certain situations may require the initiation of a specific procedure when the bond has been lost or destroyed.

Several documents may be useful in establishing the existence of a capitalisation bond, including subscription documents, statements or certificates issued by the financial institution, and correspondence with the bond issuer.

Payment of a bond may be subject to specific deadlines related to the contract or the rules applicable to financial products. It may be advisable to examine the applicable time limits in order to preserve the holder's rights.

In principle, the bond belongs to the person who physically holds it. This is why the loss or theft of a bond may present a risk of presentation by a third party.

When there is doubt regarding the existence or status of a bond, it may be useful to contact the issuing institution to obtain information about the contract or instrument.

When an institution refuses to settle a capitalisation bond, it may be necessary to analyse the grounds for refusal, examine the possible legal steps, and, if necessary, initiate the appropriate proceedings to enforce your rights.

The value of a capitalisation bond depends in particular on the conditions set at the time of issuance, such as the initial subscribed capital, the term of the bond, the rate of return or capitalisation method, and the maturity date. To determine the exact value, it is generally necessary to refer to the contract terms or request information from the issuing institution.
