Liability of Insurance Intermediaries

Insurance intermediaries are subject to strict professional obligations. When an intermediary fails to comply with these obligations, their liability may be engaged.

Broker Misconduct

An insurance broker may be held liable when they commit a fault in the performance of their duties.

Such liability may arise in cases of incomplete or inaccurate information, a failure to properly assess the client's needs, or an error in the subscription or management of a contract.

Financial Adviser Liability

Financial advisers or wealth management consultants may also incur liability when they are involved in the subscription or management of an insurance product.

Unsuitable Advice

Investment recommendations unsuited to the client's risk profile, investment horizon or wealth management objectives.

Misleading Presentation

Incomplete or misleading presentation of the product, omission of essential information regarding the features, fees or conditions of the contract.

Failure to Disclose Risks

Absence of explanation regarding the risks of capital loss, product liquidity or the conditions for exiting the contract.

Conflicts of Interest

Product recommendations motivated by retrocessions or personal advantages, to the detriment of the client's interest.

Failure to Advise

The duty to advise is one of the essential obligations incumbent upon insurance intermediaries. The professional must notably:

Analysis

Analyse the client's personal situation and needs.

Suitable Product

Recommend a product suited to the client's profile and objectives.

Explanation

Explain the features, risks and conditions of the contract.

Follow-up

Provide appropriate follow-up and inform the client of any significant developments.

A breach of this duty to advise may engage the intermediary's liability.
